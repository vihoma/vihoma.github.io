I have computers as a hobby and I own & run at the moment three computers:
#1 A Jimm's Business Class President (assembled in Jimm's PC Store) Xeon E3-1230v3 3,3GHz, 16GB RAM, 250GB Samsung EVO SSD drive, 1TB Western Digital Caviar Black HDD, Asus RoG STRIX GTX 1060 OC 6GB GPU, Dell U2515H 25" WQHD display, OS: Ubuntu 16.04 LTS;
#2 AMD Athlon64 3200+, 1024MB RAM, 20GB HDD, PNY Quadro 600 1GB GPU, OS: Ubuntu 16.04 (in text-mode);
#3 ICL 486SX (with a 487 math-coprocessor) 33MHz, 8MB RAM, 100MB HDD, OS: NetBSD 2.0_RC4 (headless).

The underlined #1, Business Class President is currently my desktop and "workstation", also participating in Folding@Home. #2, the Athlon64 box, is just running Folding@Home with the GPU. #3, 486SX worked before as a firewall/router for my ADSL-connection, afterwards it became my LAN's secondary nameserver (just because I can ;)) and it's still running fine. I took the little devil offline, but after a while I took it back to use as the DNS server on my LAN.

I also play a little with virtual machines, having guest operating systems like OpenBSD (whatever is the latest stable release), Minix 3, FreeDOS. I test whatever that perks my interest from the world of open source operating systems.
