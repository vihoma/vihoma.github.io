I have computers as a hobby and I own & run at the moment four machines:
#1 A Jimm's Business Class President (assembled initially in Jimm's PC Store) Xeon E3-1230v3 3,3GHz, 32GB RAM, 250GB Samsung EVO SSD storage, 1TB Western Digital Caviar Black HDD, Asus RoG STRIX GTX 1060 OC 6GB GPU, additional Gigabit PCI-e Intel Pro Desktop NIC dedicated for network packet sniffing with the Security Onion VM, and a Dell U3415W 34" Ultawide QHD display, OS: Ubuntu 16.04 LTS. Runs 24/7 and has some Virtual Machines with services always-on;
#2 A Raspberry Pi 3 B running Unbound, a forwarding DNS server for my LAN and for Irssi (IRC client), OS: Raspbian (latest stable release, "Stretch" at the moment);
#3 A LibVirt/KVM VM with a Ubuntu 14.04 LTS based NIDS/HIDS/NSM/SIEM distro named Security Onion. Has 4 3.3GHz Haswsell-NoTSX cores, 12GB RAM, 500GB raw-format virtio bus mass storage and two virtualized & brigded Intel Pro GBE NICs, one for network connection, the other dedicated for packet sniffing. Has for example Suricata, Bro, Netsniff-NG (pcap), OSSEC, Sguild, ELSA and other network security tools and web-based user interfaces for monitoring and analysis. I also connect with Sguil from my workstation for SIEM (I guess that's what it is called?);
#4 A LibVirt/KVM VM with OpenBSD-stable for Unbound, a forwarding DNS server for my LAN. Has 1 3.3GHz Haswell-NoTSX core, 512MB RAM, 8GB qcow2-format with virtio bus mass storage and bridged virtio networking;

The mentioned virtual machines run 24/7, suspending on workstation shutdowns and reboots and resuming at workstations OS boot

Archery is a new hobby of mine and I joined Puijo Archery Club, (or in Finnish "Puijon Jousi ry") in April 2018. I didn't hesitate to get myself a hunting compound bow before even joining the club and without asking the local archers about choosing a suitable bow, silly me ;) Well, I'm content with my choise, a black Diamond Edge SB-1 set to 29.5" draw length and 45lb draw weight. Will be upgrading the arrow shelf, now a basic Octane Hostage designed for hunting, to a target shooters shelf once I get a hang of the form, the bow, the draw and the release.
New target bow!!!, a red Diamond Medalist 38 , sold the Diamond Edge SB-1 and got a new rig, with equipment designed for target archery like the arrow rest, sight, scope, stabilizers and a smaller interchangeable diameter peep sight. This thing helps me shoot more consistent and accurate for sure, not underminding the archers part in the process though! Anyways, scores especially at 50m for 60 arrows have improved by take or leave 150 points after the swap to this new bow and other rig i got with it. For now I've found archery to be very relaxing, enhancing my focus at least for the time I'm shooting and for some time after a practice session. When all goes well one can find a really calm place while shooting, it's like there's nothing else but the excersise and even the missed or failed shots go by and a new arrow takes flight.I also feel my posture has become better, i can keep my shoulders with poise. Archery definately helps with posture, one needs to use the muscles at and around scapulae. Only "problem" I had with this hobby was aching fingers and finger joints from pulling the arrows from the target backings made from laminated rubber foam. Even at 45lb the arrows seem to burn tightly and deep to the backing, no matter if I apply some silicone to them or not, so an arrow puller is a must it seems (which I've acquired now and it helps). I think I finally found a hobby which i can enjoy for the rest of my life with joy and interest. What a rush this archery is for me!
